= 2D Translation

Translating a point with <</reference/math/homogeneous-coordinates/explanations#,homogeneous coordinates>> stem:[(x, y, w)] by a vector stem:[(\Delta x, \Delta y, 0)] can be computed using the following matrix multiplication:

[stem]
++++
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & \Delta x \\
0 & 1 & \Delta y \\
0 & 0 & 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
\cdot
\begin{bmatrix}
x \\
y \\
w \\
\end{bmatrix}
++++

Note that translation only has any effect on points (stem:[w = 1]): vectors (stem:[w = 0]) remain unaffected.

[NOTE,caption="Example"]
====
Translating stem:[P(3, 5)] by stem:[\vec v(2,-4)]:

[stem]
++++
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 2 \\
0 & 1 & -4 \\
0 & 0 & 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
\cdot
\begin{bmatrix}
3 \\
5 \\
1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
4 \\
-1 \\
1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
++++
====

[NOTE,caption="Example"]
====
Translating stem:[\vec n(0, 1)] by stem:[\vec v(2,-4)]:

[stem]
++++
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 2 \\
0 & 1 & -4 \\
0 & 0 & 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
\cdot
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
1 \\
0 \\
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
1 \\
0 \\
\end{bmatrix}
++++

Notice how translating a vector has no effect.
