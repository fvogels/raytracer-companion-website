= Lissajous Animation

== 2D

Just for giggles, let's see what happens if we use stem:[\sin] and stem:[\cos] to make 2D animations.

[stem]
++++
\begin{array}{rcl}
  x(t) & = & \cos(2\pi t) \\
  y(t) & = & \sin(2\pi t) \\
\end{array}
++++

where stem:[t] varies from stem:[0] to stem:[1].

image::circle.png[align="center",width="50%"]

So, using that formula, we can make a shape move around in circles.

We can generalize these formulae to

[stem]
++++
\begin{array}{rcl}
  x(t) & = & A \cdot \sin(B \cdot 2\pi \cdot t - C) \\
  y(t) & = & D \cdot \sin(E \cdot 2\pi \cdot t) \\
\end{array}
++++

where different choices for stem:[A], stem:[B], stem:[C], stem:[D] and stem:[E] will yield different results.

== 3D

We can do the same for three dimensions:

[stem]
++++
\begin{array}{rcl}
  x(t) & = & A \cdot \sin(B \cdot 2\pi \cdot t) \\
  y(t) & = & C \cdot \sin(D \cdot 2\pi \cdot t - E) \\
  z(t) & = & F \cdot \sin(G \cdot 2\pi \cdot t - H) \\
\end{array}
++++
